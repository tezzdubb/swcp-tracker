SWCP Tracker

An Android app for ticking off the South West Coast Path, one section at a time.

I'm walking the Coast Path the way most people actually walk it: in bits, over more weekends than I first planned. I wanted a simple way to keep score, couldn't find one I liked, so I built this. It's the first app I've ever made, which feels appropriate for a path that's mostly about stubbornly carrying on.

It does one job. You walk a section, you tick it off, and a green line creeps a little further around the peninsula. There's a journal for each section if you want to remember where the good pasty was, and that's about it.

Progress, the 52 sections, the map, and a section page. Yes, I've only done five. It's a long path.

What it does

  • All 52 official sections, Minehead to South Haven Point, with distances and difficulty. Tick them off whole, or stretch-by-stretch between villages if you're doing it in short days.
  • The whole trail on a proper outdoor map. Walked bits in green, the rest in grey. Tiles you've looked at keep working with no signal, which matters on most of the path.
  • A journal per section: date walked, notes, photos.
  • Backup to a single file, restore on any phone. That's the whole sync strategy.

The signs say 630 miles. OpenStreetMap, which the app's route data comes from, measures 642.9. The app shows you both and lets you argue with whichever you prefer.

It keeps itself to itself

No account, no ads, no analytics. I have no server and I wouldn't know what to do with your data if I had it. Everything lives on your phone unless you export it yourself. The privacy policy is eight short paragraphs mostly saying that in different ways.

Getting it

It's nearly on Google Play. New developers have to run a closed test first, so if you fancy being an early tester (and getting the app now), open an issue on GitHub and I'll send you the opt-in link. It'll be free either way.

The tip jar

The app is free and staying free. If it earns its keep on your walk you can buy me a coffee. Better yet, chuck a few quid at the South West Coast Path Association instead. They fix the steps you'll swear at on the way down to Speke's Mill.

— Tom

SWCP Tracker is an unofficial hobby project with no connection to the South West Coast Path Association or National Trails. It's a logbook, not a navigation tool: carry a map, and check tides, ferry seasons and the Lulworth firing times before you set out.
